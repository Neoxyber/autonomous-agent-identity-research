# Code of Conduct

## Purpose

This repository is a public research project on autonomous agent identity.

The project welcomes serious technical discussion, careful criticism, and research contributions that improve clarity, security, evaluation, or implementation quality.

## Expected conduct

Participants are expected to:

1. Communicate respectfully.

2. Focus criticism on ideas, evidence, designs, and implementation details.

3. Avoid personal attacks, harassment, discrimination, or abusive language.

4. Be clear when making claims, assumptions, or recommendations.

5. Respect the research nature of the project and avoid promotional or misleading language.

6. Avoid sharing secrets, private keys, credentials, personal data, or confidential material.

## Research standards

Contributions should support the purpose of the repository.

The project values:

1. Clear reasoning.

2. Evidence-based discussion.

3. Honest limitations.

4. Reproducible testing.

5. Security-aware design.

6. Careful use of references.

## Unacceptable conduct

The following behaviour is not acceptable:

1. Harassment or abusive communication.

2. Discriminatory language or conduct.

3. Attempts to use the project to hide agent activity, impersonate users, bypass access control, or weaken audit trails.

4. Publishing secrets, private credentials, or sensitive personal information.

5. Deliberately misleading claims about security, compliance, or production readiness.

## Reporting concerns

Concerns may be reported privately to:

security@aixybertech.com

Reports should include a clear description of the concern and any relevant links, files, or context.

## Enforcement

The maintainers may remove comments, reject contributions, restrict participation, or take other reasonable action to protect the quality and safety of the project.
